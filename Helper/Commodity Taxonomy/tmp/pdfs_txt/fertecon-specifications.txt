          Specifications Guide              Fertecon: January 2026
          Definitions of the trading locations for which Platts publishes indexes or assessments
          The following specifications guide contains the primary specifications for S&P Global Energy’s Platts global fertilizer assessments. All the assessments listed here employ Platts Assessments Methodology, 
          as published at https://www.spglobal.com/commodityinsights/plattscontent/_assets/_files/en/our-methodology/methodology-specifications/platts-assessments-methodology-guide.pdf
          These guides are designed to give our subscribers as much information as possible about a wide range of methodology and specification questions.
          This guide is current at the time of publication. S&P Global may issue further updates and enhancements to this guide and will announce these to subscribers through its usual publications of record. 
          Such updates will be included in the next version of this guide. Our editorial staff and managers are available to provide guidance when assessment issues require clarification.
          The following global fertilizers guide contains the primary specifications and methodologies for S&P Global fertilizer assessments throughout the world. The various components of this guide are 
          designed to give our subscribers as much information as possible about a wide range of methodology and specification issues.
          Fertecon’s individual fertilizer market assessments can cover a broad range of load ports or markets, product grades or other specifications. Where this is the case, these differences are captured in the 
          published low-high range for the market in question. The low-high range does not represent different tradeable values for product with the same specifications. 
          Any calculated FOB netbacks fertilizer and minerals assessments that are based on FOB US assessments will also account for the subtraction of the prevailing tariff, where relevant. Where these 
          netbacks are published as ranges, incorporating multiple origins, any difference in duty rates between the countries of origin will be accounted for in the published range.
          Any FOB netbacks based on CFR US assessments will not include any additional subtraction of duty, since customs clearance is not included in the original assessment. 
          Fertecon weekly/bi-weekly market assessments are determined from information gathered during the period prior to publication of the report up to 16.30 Penang, London, or Sao Paulo (timestamp 
          depending on assessment) on the day of publication, with greatest weight given to inputs received nearest to the close of the assessment period. Market information received after this time will 
          contribute to the assessments published in the following full publication.
